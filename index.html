Software Developer

After receiving a Physics Degree from the University of Michigan in 1997, I began teaching Physics in the city of Detroit. My early experiences there, seeing the vast disparities between communities with and without resources, led to a lifelong interest in issues of equity and access for under-represented populations in higher education.

After periods teaching in Germany and going back to U of M to pursue studies in Higher Education, I found myself teaching again at an urban school, this time in Kalamazoo, MI, and this is where I spent the majority of my teaching career.

In addition to the daily thrill of physics problem-solving, my career has offered the opportunity to continually work to understand and impact larger global issues alongside my students. Dewey, the great philosopher of education, described a school as a place to develop democratic citizens, and a democratic citizen is one who not only participates in democracy as it is, but who strives to make it what it could and should become.
